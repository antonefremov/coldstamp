How ColdStamp works

ColdStamp is a Chrome extension that does two separate things at checkout. Both run entirely on your device.

1. The warning panel

When you visit a checkout page, ColdStamp quietly scans it for patterns associated with hidden charges:

  • A recurring or auto-renewing option that is already ticked for you
  • A free trial that quietly converts to a paid plan
  • A recurring price shown in much smaller or fainter type than the headline
  • An order total that is bigger than the items add up to
  • A "no" button worded to make you feel bad for declining

When one of these is found, a small panel appears in the corner of the page. The panel is informational — it does not change the page, submit anything, or interfere with checkout. You can dismiss it.

The panel runs without saving anything about you. It works whether or not you have turned on evidence capture.

2. The evidence record

When you click a primary checkout button — Subscribe, Start free trial, Place order — ColdStamp captures a redacted snapshot of the page at that moment. This is your own record of what the page actually showed and what you agreed to.

The snapshot contains:

  • Which form fields existed and which boxes were ticked
  • A timestamped log of your clicks and scrolls on the page
  • The amount and currency of any payment request the page sent
  • A screenshot of the visible part of the page
  • A SHA-256 fingerprint of the whole thing, so any later tampering is detectable

This feature is off by default and only runs after you turn it on.

The snapshot does not contain:

  • Your card number or CVV — these are replaced with [REDACTED] before anything is stored
  • The raw payment request body — only a hash of it is kept
  • Any data about pages outside checkouts

What leaves your device

Nothing. Everything ColdStamp captures stays in your browser's local storage. There is no ColdStamp server, no account, no analytics. You can confirm this by reading the source code (link below).

You can export any snapshot as a JSON file from the extension popup and share it however you like — for example, attaching it to a chargeback claim. That export is the first time any record leaves your device, and you decide who to send it to.

Why this exists

Subscription vendors keep structured, timestamped records of every signup. When something goes wrong, they show up to disputes with logs and screenshots. You show up with a vague memory.

ColdStamp is the user's side of that record. It does not chase down the vendor for you, does not file disputes, does not connect to your bank. It just makes sure that when you need to prove what the page actually said, you can.
